# M1.16 Mission Control Capital Health Planning

**Status:** Planning checkpoint active  
**Canonical branch:** `main`  
**Source of truth:** GitHub repository `markperezne8-spec/MarketDEX`  
**Issue:** #497  
**Parent checkpoint:** `docs/checkpoints/M1.15E-todays-top3-checkpoint-sync.md`

## Milestone intent

M1.16 defines the next safe Mission Control North Star milestone after the completed M1.15 `Today's Top 3` sequence: a contract-first path for a read-only `Capital Health` surface.

The future surface should eventually answer: `Is business capital available, recycling, committed, and growing?`

This planning checkpoint authorizes planning only. It does not implement UI, calculate capital metrics, display fake values, connect financial providers, execute actions, mutate business data, add automation, or introduce live checks.

## Current gap from the North Star

M1.14 mapped `Capital Health` as a contract-first North Star region because no approved desktop capital view-model exists yet.

The approved Capital Health direction exists in repository authority:

- Capital Health is organized around `Availability`, `Recycling`, `Commitment`, and `Growth`;
- Available Cash remains distinct from Available for Redeployment;
- Capital Recycling Rate is protected and remains unresolved at the exact formula level;
- Capital Growth measures business-cycle growth, not external cash injection;
- Capital Health should explain dimensions rather than collapse them into one opaque score.

## Safe planning principle

M1.16 treats Capital Health as an explanation contract, not as permission to invent financial totals.

Future implementation may show real Capital Health values only after an immutable view-model or service boundary defines:

- the local-only evidence accepted by the surface;
- the deterministic grouping of Availability, Recycling, Commitment, and Growth;
- plain-language unavailable, partial, ready, and error-safe states;
- the distinction between cash available now and capital available for redeployment;
- the evidence required before any recycling, commitment, or growth value can appear;
- how external capital injections are excluded from business-cycle growth;
- how formula-unresolved values remain unavailable rather than guessed.

## Approved future evidence posture

M1.16 future slices may consume only prepared or injected local evidence. Missing evidence must produce `Unavailable` or `Partial` output rather than inferred live status.

Candidate future evidence categories, once explicitly contracted:

- local cash or cash-equivalent evidence already approved for application use;
- settled sale, payout, or completed transaction evidence from approved local records;
- inventory cost and capital commitment evidence from approved Inventory boundaries;
- purchase, receipt, grading, transformation, sale, settlement, payout, and exception events once approved for capital attribution;
- prepared local period context such as 30 / 90 / 365 / all-history windows.

Excluded until separately approved:

- financial-provider integration;
- online bank, card, brokerage, or payment-provider access;
- invented cash balances;
- live marketplace prices;
- unapproved capital-recycling formulas;
- automatic accounting conclusions;
- alerts, notifications, or scheduled checks.

## Planned deterministic state model

The future contract should preserve these states:

- `Ready` - sufficient local evidence supports a read-only capital dimension;
- `Unavailable` - required evidence or formula authority is missing;
- `Partial` - some local evidence exists, but the dimension cannot be treated as complete;
- `Error-safe` - injected evidence or assembly fails and the UI must render inline safe text.

State labels must be deterministic and plain-language. Error-safe rendering must remain inline and non-modal.

## Planned display contract

The future visible surface should remain compact and read-only. Candidate groups:

- `Availability` - Available Cash and Available for Redeployment as separate concepts;
- `Recycling` - Capital Recycling Rate only after formula authority exists;
- `Commitment` - capital currently committed to inventory, grading, purchases, exceptions, or other approved local buckets;
- `Growth` - business-cycle capital growth that excludes external cash injection.

Candidate item fields:

- group label;
- status;
- value label or unavailable reason;
- period label when applicable;
- evidence summary;
- source authority;
- freshness or as-of label when available;
- plain-language explanation.

The display must not include action buttons, launch workflows, command execution, popups, alerts, notifications, automatic remediation, or direct mutation controls.

## Relationship to existing Mission Control surfaces

M1.16 should preserve existing accepted Mission Control order and behavior.

`Capital Health` should complement the completed `Today's Top 3` surface:

- `Today's Top 3` answers: `What deserves attention first today?`
- `Capital Health` answers: `What is the current capital condition of the business?`

Future Capital Health values may inform future attention items only through a separately approved evidence contract.

## Suggested mergeable build sequence

### M1.16A - Capital Health view-model contract

Create a non-visual immutable view model for the `Capital Health` surface. It should accept prepared local evidence through injection only, preserve deterministic group ordering, and render missing evidence as `Unavailable` or `Partial`.

### M1.16B - First visible Capital Health shell

Place a compact read-only `Capital Health` surface in Mission Control using the M1.16A contract. Default missing evidence should render honest unavailable groups. This visible build requires screenshot review.

### M1.16C - Deterministic display states

Lock `Ready`, `Unavailable`, `Partial`, and `Error-safe` visual rendering for capital groups, including inline error-safe text and deterministic ordering.

### M1.16D - Visual and contract verification

Record screenshot evidence, placement verification, read-only behavior, injected-only behavior, deterministic ordering, absence of action controls, and absence of popup, polling, networking, persistence, automation, alert, notification, financial-provider, formula-invention, or business mutation paths.

### M1.16E - Checkpoint and documentation sync

Synchronize repository authority after accepted visual evidence and CI have passed.

Each build must remain independently reviewable and mergeable.

## Visual acceptance criteria

Future visible M1.16 builds require screenshot review. Acceptance should confirm:

- the surface is visibly aligned with the North Star `Capital Health` concept;
- unavailable evidence is labeled honestly;
- Availability, Recycling, Commitment, and Growth remain distinct;
- Available Cash remains distinct from Available for Redeployment;
- existing accepted Mission Control surfaces remain present and usable;
- no fake cash values, fake capital values, executable actions, financial-provider integrations, alerts, notifications, polling, networking, persistence changes, or business mutation are introduced.

M1.16 planning is complete when the repository records the contract-first path for `Capital Health`, deterministic state expectations, evidence boundaries, explicit non-goals, staged build sequence, and visual acceptance criteria.

No runtime behavior, UI implementation, financial-provider integration, marketplace integration, alerts, notifications, polling, network access, persistence, database migration, task execution, capital calculation, formula invention, or business mutation is authorized by this planning checkpoint.
