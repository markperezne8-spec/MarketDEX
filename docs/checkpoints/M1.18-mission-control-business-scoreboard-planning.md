# M1.18 Mission Control Business Scoreboard Planning

**Status:** Planning checkpoint active
**Canonical branch:** `main`
**Source of truth:** GitHub repository `markperezne8-spec/MarketDEX`
**Issue:** #522
**Parent checkpoint:** `docs/checkpoints/M1.17E-opportunity-risk-checkpoint-sync.md`

## Milestone intent

M1.18 defines the next safe Mission Control North Star milestone after the completed M1.17 `Opportunity + Risk` sequence: a contract-first path for a read-only `Business Scoreboard` surface.

The future surface should eventually answer: `How is the business performing during the selected period?`

This planning checkpoint authorizes planning only. It does not implement UI, calculate real scoreboard values, display fake KPI values, alter accounting or settlement meaning, execute actions, mutate business data, or introduce live checks.

## Current gap from the North Star

M1.14 mapped `Business Scoreboard` as a contract-first North Star region because the desktop app has existing local KPI cards but no approved scoreboard grouping, immutable view-model, or calculation-meaning contract for the full scoreboard.

The approved Business Scoreboard direction exists in repository authority:

- Business Scoreboard is a concise outcome-summary zone;
- it answers period performance, not current capital condition;
- it should group money outcomes separately from throughput outcomes;
- the controlled 30 DAYS / 90 DAYS / 365 DAYS / ALL HISTORY selector applies only where mathematically appropriate;
- the default initial Mission Control period remains 90 DAYS;
- derived KPI values are not manual-entry responsibilities in Mission Control;
- exact calculation meanings remain subject to a formal Business Scoreboard Calculation Meaning Contract.

## Safe planning principle

M1.18 treats Business Scoreboard as a read-only outcome-summary contract, not as permission to invent formulas, accounting behavior, or analytics.

Future implementation may show real scoreboard values only after an immutable view-model or service boundary defines:

- the local-only evidence accepted by the surface;
- the exact metric identities and labels;
- the selected period and period applicability for each metric;
- deterministic money and throughput group ordering;
- plain-language unavailable, partial, ready, and error-safe states;
- how missing, stale, conflicting, incomplete, or inapplicable evidence renders honestly;
- the calculation authority for each metric;
- source authority and as-of or period labels;
- how completed orders, settlements, fees, costs, refunds, adjustments, capital return, listing dates, sale dates, and partial evidence are handled.

## Approved future metric direction

Future M1.18 slices may plan around these approved initial metric-card concepts:

Money:

- `Net Sales Proceeds` or `Seller Net Proceeds`;
- `Net Business Gain`;
- `Capital Recycled This Period`.

Throughput:

- `Orders Completed`;
- `Items Sold`;
- `Median Days to Sell`.

The exact label choice and calculation meaning for each metric must be locked before real values display. M1.18 planning does not choose formulas or reconcile naming conflicts beyond preserving the approved direction.

## Approved future evidence posture

M1.18 future slices may consume only prepared or injected local evidence. Missing evidence must produce `Unavailable`, `Partial`, or `Not applicable` output rather than inferred revenue, profit, capital recycling, order volume, item volume, or sell-speed values.

Candidate future evidence categories, once explicitly contracted:

- completed order evidence already approved for application use;
- settlement evidence and seller net proceeds evidence;
- attributed fee, shipping, packaging, refund, adjustment, and cost-basis evidence;
- completed inventory movement and sale-date evidence;
- first active listing date evidence for sell-speed observations;
- period selection evidence;
- source authority, confidence, as-of, and period labels.

Excluded until separately approved:

- formula invention;
- manual KPI entry;
- accounting-sheet behavior;
- sales-history table behavior;
- Analytics replacement behavior;
- Capital Health duplication;
- live marketplace polling;
- marketplace or live-pricing integration;
- financial-provider integration;
- alerts or notifications;
- background workers or scheduled checks;
- fake sales, gain, capital-recycled, order, item, or sell-speed values;
- Buy, Sell, grade, list, purchase, ship, payout, settlement, or reconciliation commands.

## Planned deterministic state model

The future contract should preserve these states:

- `Ready` - sufficient prepared local evidence supports a read-only metric or group;
- `Unavailable` - required evidence is missing;
- `Partial` - some local evidence exists, but the metric or group cannot be treated as complete;
- `Not applicable` - the selected period or evidence shape does not mathematically apply to the metric;
- `Error-safe` - injected evidence or assembly fails and the UI must render inline safe text.

State labels must be deterministic and plain-language. Error-safe rendering must remain inline and non-modal.

## Planned display contract

The future visible surface should remain compact and read-only.

Candidate groups:

- `Money` - Net Sales Proceeds or Seller Net Proceeds, Net Business Gain, and Capital Recycled This Period;
- `Throughput` - Orders Completed, Items Sold, and Median Days to Sell.

Candidate metric fields:

- metric label;
- state label;
- value label;
- period label;
- evidence summary;
- source authority;
- calculation authority or unavailable reason;
- future OPEN routing toward authoritative detail without turning Mission Control into an editor.

The display must not include action buttons, manual KPI inputs, launch workflows, command execution, popups, alerts, notifications, automatic remediation, or direct mutation controls.

## Relationship to existing Mission Control surfaces

M1.18 should preserve existing accepted Mission Control order and behavior.

Business Scoreboard should complement completed surfaces:

- `Today's Top 3` answers: `What deserves attention first today?`
- `Capital Health` answers: `What is the current capital condition of the business?`
- `Opportunity + Risk` answers: `Where may advantage or downside change if Mark waits?`
- `Business Scoreboard` answers: `How is the business performing during the selected period?`

Business Scoreboard must not duplicate Capital Health. Capital Recycled This Period may appear only after a formal calculation authority explains how it differs from availability, redeployment, recycling rate, and growth.

## Suggested mergeable build sequence

### M1.18A - Business Scoreboard view-model contract

Create a non-visual immutable view model for the `Business Scoreboard` surface. It should accept prepared local evidence through injection only, preserve deterministic Money/Throughput ordering, preserve period labels, and render missing evidence as `Unavailable`, `Partial`, or `Not applicable`.

### M1.18B - First visible Business Scoreboard shell

Place a compact read-only `Business Scoreboard` surface in Mission Control using the M1.18A contract. Default missing evidence should render honest unavailable Money and Throughput groups. This visible build requires screenshot review.

### M1.18C - Deterministic display states

Lock `Ready`, `Unavailable`, `Partial`, `Not applicable`, and `Error-safe` visual rendering for Business Scoreboard groups and metrics, including inline error-safe text and deterministic ordering.

### M1.18D - Visual and contract verification

Record screenshot evidence, placement verification, read-only behavior, injected-only behavior, deterministic ordering, period-label behavior, absence of manual KPI entry, absence of action controls, and absence of popup, polling, networking, persistence, automation, alert, notification, provider, fake-value, formula-invention, command, or business mutation paths.

### M1.18E - Checkpoint and documentation sync

Synchronize repository authority after accepted visual evidence and CI have passed.

Each build must remain independently reviewable and mergeable.
