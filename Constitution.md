# Article I

## 🎯 Purpose

The Constitution defines the principles that guide every major decision
made throughout the MarketDEX project.

Its purpose is to protect the long-term vision of MarketDEX while
allowing the project to evolve responsibly through real business
experience.

The Constitution exists to provide consistency.

Whenever uncertainty exists, the Constitution should be consulted before
making significant decisions.

The Constitution is intended to outlive any specific product category,
technology, programming language, framework, database, or platform.

As products, markets, and technology evolve, the principles contained
within this document should continue to guide MarketDEX.

Every future worksheet, prototype, report, client, module, workflow, and
architectural decision should remain consistent with this Constitution.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Article II

## 🧭 Core Principles

Every major decision made throughout MarketDEX should support one or
more of the following principles.

### Principle 1 --- 💼 Business Before Technology

Technology exists to support the business.

Business requirements and real operational needs should guide technical
decisions.

MarketDEX should never adopt technology simply because it is new,
popular, or impressive.

### Principle 2 --- 🧠 Knowledge Before Technology

Knowledge is the true product of MarketDEX.

The workbook captures and refines knowledge. Real business experience
challenges knowledge. Future prototypes may visualize knowledge. Future
software may implement knowledge.

Technology may change. Valuable business understanding should remain.

### Principle 3 --- 📊 Workbook First

The LibreOffice workbook is the primary active product and first working
edition of MarketDEX.

Major business workflows should be understood, tested, and refined
through the workbook and real business use before future software
implementation becomes an active priority.

The workbook is not disposable. It is a permanent business operating
system, knowledge asset, and continuity backup.

### Principle 4 --- 👀 Comprehension Before Implementation

Understanding should come before implementation.

MarketDEX should first understand the business question, workflow,
information, and decision being supported.

Future prototypes or software should implement proven understanding
rather than invent business logic during development.

### Principle 5 --- 🌱 Build Gradually

MarketDEX should evolve through small, thoughtful improvements.

Long-term quality is preferred over rushed expansion.

Capability should be added when meaningful business value justifies it.

### Principle 6 --- ✨ Comprehension Before Complexity

The easiest system to understand is usually the easiest system to
improve.

Complexity should only exist when it provides meaningful value.

Whenever two solutions provide similar value, prefer the simpler
solution.

### Principle 7 --- 📁 One Source of Truth

Information should exist in one authoritative location whenever
practical.

Duplicate information should be minimized.

Repository knowledge should preserve approved project direction.

Workbook data and business rules should have clearly defined
authoritative locations.

### Principle 8 --- 👤 Human First

MarketDEX should reduce mental workload.

Every improvement should make the business easier to understand, easier
to operate, or easier to decide within.

The system should support human judgment rather than bury it beneath
data or complexity.

### Principle 9 --- 👀 Visual Comprehension

MarketDEX should transform information into visual understanding
whenever practical.

The preferred comprehension flow is:

> **👀 Scan → 😊 Recognize → 📊 Understand → 📖 Read details only when
> necessary**

Charts, graphs, percentages, KPI-style summaries, comparisons, progress
indicators, and status indicators should be used when they improve
understanding.

Visuals should never be added only for decoration.

### Principle 10 --- 🧩 One Unified System

MarketDEX should support different collectibles and gaming categories
without becoming a collection of disconnected systems.

🃏 Pokémon TCG, 🐉 Dragon Ball Card Game, and 🧸 Funko Pop may have
different products, terminology, and workflows.

MarketDEX should respect meaningful differences while preserving shared
business knowledge, rules, and workflows whenever practical.

Different collectibles.

One business operating system.

### Principle 11 --- 🧭 Adaptable by Design

MarketDEX should remain adaptable to future product categories, business
growth, and technologies when they provide meaningful value.

The system should not overbuild for an imagined future.

The foundation should not unnecessarily trap MarketDEX inside today's
business size or product scope.

Expansion should be deliberate.

### Principle 12 --- 🛡️ Protect the Foundation

The foundation should remain stable.

Future versions should expand and refine the foundation rather than
constantly replacing it.

Changes to foundational direction should come from meaningful new
knowledge, real-world experience, or justified architecture review.

### Principle 13 --- 🌍 Reality Has the Final Vote

MarketDEX should be shaped by real business experience.

Assumptions may begin a design. Experience should refine it.

When real-world use contradicts a workflow, calculation, report, or
design assumption, MarketDEX should review and improve the system.

Reality has the final vote.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Principle 14 — 🗄️ Preserve Meaningful History

MarketDEX should preserve meaningful historical information so years of business activity, inventory, collection, market observations, decisions, and outcomes can become future business intelligence.

Useful history should not be overwritten simply because newer information exists.

Historical knowledge should be deleted only through intentional decisions when practical.

The Knowledge Warehouse should become more valuable as MarketDEX accumulates real experience.

# Article III

## 🎯 Business Philosophy

MarketDEX exists to support real business and collection decisions
across collectibles and gaming.

The current active product categories are:

-   🃏 Pokémon TCG
-   🐉 Dragon Ball Card Game
-   🧸 Funko Pop

🎮 Gaming and additional collectible categories remain future
possibilities.

Every feature, worksheet, report, workflow, visualization, and future
software module should help answer one or more meaningful business
questions.

The purpose of MarketDEX is not to collect information.

The purpose is to transform information into understanding.

Understanding should lead to confidence.

Confidence should support better decisions.

Better decisions should strengthen the business and collection.

## 🎯 Decision Driven

Every part of MarketDEX should help answer questions such as:

-   📦 What do I own?
-   🛒 What should I buy?
-   💵 What should I sell?
-   🔍 What deserves more research?
-   💎 What is my inventory worth?
-   ❤️ What is my collection worth?
-   📈 What changed?
-   🎯 What should I do next?

If a feature does not help answer an important business question, it
should be reconsidered.

## 🧸 Collector and Business First

MarketDEX should respect both the collector and the business operator.

It should help organize collections with the same care that it helps
manage inventory, purchases, sales, and financial understanding.

Business and collecting should work together rather than compete.

Different product categories may require different collection or
business details.

Those differences should be supported only when they create meaningful
value.

## 🧩 One Business System

MarketDEX should never become a collection of disconnected tools.

📦 Inventory

🔍 Research

🛒 Purchases

💵 Sales

❤️ Collection

📋 Reports

📊 Analytics

Every component should work together as one integrated operating system.

Product categories should share common business systems whenever
practical.

Category-specific workflows should exist only when the product type
genuinely requires them.

## 🧠 Comprehension Is Success

More data is not success.

More charts are not success.

More features are not success.

MarketDEX succeeds when the user understands the business better and can
make stronger decisions with less unnecessary effort.

Information should become understanding.

Understanding should become action.

## 🔄 Continuous Improvement

The business will evolve.

The collectibles market will evolve.

MarketDEX should evolve with them.

Every improvement should make the system more useful, more
understandable, or easier to operate.

Knowledge should be created once and improved over time whenever
practical.

### Principle 15 — 🌐 Preserve Source-Aware Market Evidence

External market observations should preserve source identity, observation time, value meaning, condition or grade when relevant, region, currency, and collection method.

Raw observations are historical evidence.

Derived intelligence is interpretation.

MarketDEX should preserve the difference.

### Principle 16 — 📈 Validate Market Movement Before Action

MarketDEX should validate attention against supply, product population, transaction volume, price behavior, duration, appropriate source agreement, and market context before treating an emerging trend as decision-relevant intelligence.

Suspicious patterns may be flagged for review.

MarketDEX should not claim market manipulation without sufficient evidence.

### Principle 17 — 🔗 Preserve Learning Chains

MarketDEX should preserve the chain between observation, decision, action, and outcome whenever that chain creates future learning value.

Important decisions, experiments, seasonal plays, and market theories may use selective Decision IDs.

### Principle 18 — ⚙️ Automate Carefully and Preserve the Audit Trail

Automate data collection when reliable and permitted.

Preserve manual input as a fallback.

Never make MarketDEX knowledge dependent on one external source or connector.

Every meaningful automated observation should remain traceable from source to raw evidence, validation, interpretation, decision, and eventual outcome whenever future learning value exists.

### Principle 19 — 📊 Prove the Business System in LibreOffice

During the Version 0 workbook phase, approved MarketDEX business knowledge should be represented in LibreOffice as a worksheet, table, calculation, visual, workflow, historical record, control rule, or documented workbook automation requirement whenever reasonably possible.

Use reliable LibreOffice automation and import abilities where useful.

Preserve manual input as fallback.

### Principle 20 — 👀 Visuals Must Explain Business Meaning

Charts, percentages, conditional formatting, and attention indicators should improve comprehension.

Every important visual should answer a business question.

Visuals should explain trend, comparison, proportion, support, risk, or attention.

Charts should not exist only for decoration.

### Principle 21 — 🆔 Separate Product Knowledge from Owned Asset History

A Product identifies what an item fundamentally is.

An Asset or Lot identifies inventory Mark owns.

Market variants, external source identities, assets, lots, and historical observations should connect to stable Product IDs without duplicating core product knowledge.

### Principle 22 — 🧬 Preserve Transformation and Cost Lineage

When inventory is split, broken, bundled, graded, or otherwise transformed, MarketDEX should preserve what the inventory came from, what it became, and how original cost was allocated.

Cost allocation methods should remain auditable.

### Principle 23 — ❤️ Collection Decisions Remain Human Decisions

Collection intelligence may indicate Keep, Watch, or Consider Selling.

Market evidence may inform a collection decision.

Personal importance, collection goals, and opportunity cost may also matter.

MarketDEX should not treat personal collection items as ordinary resale inventory or automatically decide to sell them.

### Principle 24 — 🏪 Measure Platform Fit

Marketplace-specific costs, listing activity, sale outcomes, and performance history should be preserved so platform selection becomes a measurable business decision.

MarketDEX should not assume which platform is best.

Historical business outcomes should answer the question.

### Principle 25 — 🧮 Separate Historical Records from Heavy Calculation Responsibility

Historical worksheets should preserve authoritative records.

Calculation responsibilities should be separated when repeated formulas, large datasets, or complex analysis would reduce workbook clarity or performance.

### Principle 26 — 🚀 Measure Capital Scalability
Study capital efficiency, capital velocity, actual outcomes, commitment time, and reinvestment history together. Margin alone is not enough.

### Principle 27 — 📊 Evidence Support Is Not Probability
Evidence Support must remain explainable and must not be presented as probability, guaranteed recommendation, or precise future price forecast.

### Principle 28 — 🔥 Attention Creates a Hypothesis
Attention must be tested against transaction volume, observable supply, population, price, duration, source agreement, related-product movement, and market context.

### Principle 29 — 💎 Value Disconnects Trigger Research
Undervalued indicators trigger research and are not automatic Buy commands.

### Principle 30 — 🏆 Preserve Population Evidence Over Time
Timestamped grading population observations are permanent historical evidence. New observations do not overwrite old observations. Interpret Population Velocity and **Observed PSA 10 Rate** with price, volume, and raw-market context.

### Principle 31 — 🎯 Prioritize the Highest-Leverage Work
Mission Control should force-rank **Today's Top 3** items deserving Mark's review.

### Principle 32 — 🚪 Apply the Simplicity Gate
Operational space requires a clear business question, daily value, and visual value.

### Principle 33 — 👀 Charts Explain, Tables Prove, History Remembers
Visuals improve comprehension. Tables preserve inspectable evidence. History preserves what was known over time.

### Principle 34 — 🕒 Standardize Freshness
Use 🟢 CURRENT, 🟡 AGING, 🔴 STALE, and ⚪ UNKNOWN. Thresholds may differ by data family.

### Principle 35 — 💵 Preserve Capital Memory
MarketDEX should preserve how reusable business capital moves, grows, recycles, and becomes trapped over time so experience becomes visible historical evidence and a compounding decision advantage.

### Principle 36 — 🧪 Preserve Decision-Time Reality
Evidence snapshots used for meaningful decisions must preserve what was known at the decision date. Future information must not silently recalculate or rewrite historical decision evidence.

### Principle 37 — 🎯 Judge Outcomes Against the Decision Objective
Outcome evaluation should first consider the business objective that existed when the decision was made. Profit alone is not sufficient to judge every decision.

### Principle 38 — 🏆 Counterfactuals Remain Estimates
A Best Known Alternative may be preserved for learning, but it must remain clearly labeled as an estimate and must not be called the Best Decision.

### Principle 39 — 🧠 Learn Why the Framework Was Wrong
Framework errors should be classified when possible so MarketDEX can distinguish stale data, missing evidence, signal errors, weighting errors, missed context, and other causes.

### Principle 40 — ⚡ Enter Once, Understand Everywhere
Validated business events should be entered once and reused by authoritative workbook responsibilities wherever relevant.

### Principle 41 — 📚 Anticipate Future Releases
When reliable complete set data becomes available, MarketDEX should preserve the entire collectible checklist in the Product Registry. The full product universe should not depend on Mark purchasing an item first.

### Principle 42 — 🏅 Reward Real Progress
Badges may recognize defined evidence-based progress. Trophies should be reserved for major business milestones. Gamification must never pressure Mark to distort records or chase a metric over sound business judgment.

### Principle 43 — 📄 One Operational Sheet, One Major Business Question
Every operational worksheet should have one clearly defined primary business question. Supporting questions are allowed, but a sheet without a major business reason should be parked or assigned to a non-daily layer.

### Principle 45 — 💸 Preserve True Selling Economics

### Principle 46 — 🛡️ Offline-First and Subscription-Resistant

External sources, imports, connectors, and future clients may add value, but loss of one service should not erase the authoritative business history or make the core operating system unusable.

### Principle 47 — 🃏 Respect Product Form and Market Context

Sealed products, raw cards, graded cards, randomized openings, known-content breaks, promos, and other meaningful product forms may behave as different economic paths.

MarketDEX should compare them without collapsing distinct markets or double counting value.

### Principle 48 — 🔎 Human Search, Stable Identity

Mark should be able to find products using familiar human-readable identity such as product name, set, card number, character, or other meaningful descriptors.

Stable MarketDEX Product IDs remain the authoritative internal anchors.

The system should not require Mark to memorize identifiers to operate the business.

### Principle 49 — 📦 Imports Reduce Re-entry, Not Accountability

Reliable imports and prepared checklist templates should reduce repetitive work.

Imported information must still pass source, validation, identity, and review rules appropriate to the evidence.

Automation should make accurate entry easier without hiding uncertainty.

### Principle 50 — 📋 Separate Sale From Fulfillment Responsibility

A Sale Event records the financial agreement.

An Order records the operational responsibility created by that sale.

MarketDEX should preserve this distinction without forcing duplicate entry.

### Principle 51 — 🚚 Shipping Recommendations Must Balance Cost and Protection

Shipping intelligence should seek a safe and economical reasonable path using preserved evidence and controlled rules.

Version 0 should recommend rather than automatically purchase postage or pretend to be a carrier-rate engine.

### Principle 52 — ⚠️ Exceptions Must Preserve What Happened, What Changed Financially, and Current Status

Business Exception Events should distinguish Event Type, Financial Adjustment, and Operational Status.

Returns, refunds, cancellations, losses, disputes, damage, platform adjustments, and fee reversals must not silently disappear inside a final profit number.

### Principle 53 — 💰 Business Events Move Capital

Capital State should be derived from authoritative business events wherever practical.

Manual Capital State correction is an exception and should preserve reason, date, source or actor, and related event when known.

### Principle 54 — 🔍 Inventory Variance Requires Reconciliation

Physical count variance should not silently overwrite authoritative inventory quantity.

Variance should be reviewed, classified, resolved through an Inventory Adjustment Event when appropriate, and preserved historically.

### Principle 55 — 🃏 Separate Condition Observation, Platform Rules, and Grading Prediction

What Mark observes about condition is not automatically a marketplace condition rule or a grading prediction.

These responsibilities may inform one another but should remain distinguishable.

### Principle 56 — 🏷️ Listing Readiness Should Be Explainable

Listing Readiness should be derived from authoritative preparation evidence wherever practical.

When an item is not ready, MarketDEX should expose the blocking reason.

Manual override may exist but should preserve a reason.

### Principle 57 — 🧾 Marketplace Order Money Is Not Automatically Seller Revenue

Item revenue, shipping collected, marketplace-collected tax, marketplace fees, actual shipping cost, packaging cost, refunds or adjustments, and seller net proceeds should remain distinguishable.

MarketDEX should protect financial comprehension without becoming tax software.

### Principle 58 — ↩️ Returned Products Require Re-evaluation Before Sellable Inventory Restoration

A returned product should be verified and condition-reviewed before it becomes sellable inventory again.

Return receipt alone must not automatically restore sellable quantity.

### Principle 59 — 🏪 Platform Rules Must Be Effective-Dated and Freshness-Aware

Platform-specific reference rules should preserve effective dates, source reference, review date, and confidence or freshness context when appropriate.

Historical decisions should be interpreted using the rule context that existed at the time.

### Principle 60 — ⚙️ Preserve Operational Burden as Evidence Before Scoring It

Fulfillment effort may be preserved through observable evidence such as duration, order-line count, item quantity, packaging complexity, and exception occurrence.

MarketDEX should not invent a false-precision burden score merely because the evidence exists.

### Principle 61 — 🎯 Cross-Business Attention Must Be Force-Ranked

Many workbook responsibilities may identify concerns and opportunities, but one explainable Attention Engine responsibility should force-rank candidates for Today's Top 3.

MarketDEX should not allow every module to make itself urgent.

### Principle 62 — 🧠 Attention Responses Are Historical Evidence

OPEN, SNOOZE, and DISMISS responses may be preserved when useful.

Snooze means potentially relevant but not today.

Dismiss means not useful or not applicable.

These responses should not rewrite the underlying evidence.

### Principle 63 — 💰 Capital Health Must Remain Explainable

Capital Health should be understood through availability, recycling, commitment, and growth rather than one opaque mystery score.

Periods, denominators, and important exclusions should remain visible.

### Principle 64 — 🧬 Preserve Capital Origin

External capital entering the business cycle should remain distinguishable from recycled business capital, capital return, and business profit.

Capital Growth should not treat owner contribution or borrowed capital as business-cycle growth.

### Principle 65 — 📊 Compare Capital Concentration With Mark's History Before Inventing Universal Thresholds

Capital concentration may deserve attention, but Version 0 should prefer Mark's historical operating patterns over arbitrary universal thresholds when sufficient history exists.

### Principle 66 — 🖥️ Mission Control Is Primarily Read-Oriented

Mission Control should derive summaries, visuals, rankings, and indicators from authoritative responsibilities.

Mark should not manually maintain dashboard KPI values after performing the real business work elsewhere.


### Principle 67 — ⚠️ Separate Immediate Action From Developing Conditions

Today's Top 3 is the sole cross-business force-ranked actionable attention surface.

Mission Control may separately summarize developing Opportunities and Risks for situational awareness, but this responsibility must not become a second priority engine.

Exact Today's Top 3 candidates should normally be excluded from Opportunity + Risk.

A broader related condition may remain visible only when independent supporting evidence demonstrates a materially broader business pattern.

### Principle 68 — 🔄 Recycling Is Not Profit Velocity

Capital recycling and profit generation are related but distinct business meanings.

A capital cycle may return original business capital even when realized gain is zero or negative.

Capital Recycling Rate must measure explainable recycling behavior and must not silently combine return on capital and time-normalized profit into a mislabeled metric.

Time-normalized return or capital-return velocity may be studied separately when useful.

### Principle 69 — ⚡ Speed Alone Is Not a Data Anomaly

A short holding period or fast capital cycle is not inherently suspicious.

MarketDEX should validate impossible, conflicting, or unreliable dates and evidence rather than treating operational speed itself as bad data.


### Principle 70 — 👀 Visual Intelligence Reveals Patterns, Not Certainty

Mission Control Visual Intelligence exists only where a visual comparison helps Mark understand a meaningful business pattern faster.

Platform Performance compares realized Net Business Gain by platform.

Market Attention Trend keeps observable evidence families separate and may index incompatible units to a visible baseline of 100 at the selected period start.

Attention Heat Map ranks change in observable market-attention evidence rather than absolute popularity or Mark's work.

Inventory Movement compares eligible sellable inventory against Mark's historically observed comparable selling behavior when sufficient history exists.

Top Inventory Concentrations shows current eligible inventory cost-basis exposure by meaningful Set or Product Family grouping.

Visual Intelligence must not create mystery scores, BUY or SELL commands, profit probabilities, decorative charts, or duplicate authoritative responsibilities.

### Principle 71 — 📜 History Before Movement Labels

MarketDEX must not invent universal inventory-movement thresholds.

When sufficient comparable history exists, movement interpretation should use Mark's observed historical behavior for comparable inventory.

When sufficient history does not exist, MarketDEX should show Limited History and observable facts rather than false interpretation.

### Principle 72 — 🔥 Attention Change Is Not Popularity

Market attention movement is about change in observable evidence.

High absolute popularity alone does not prove attention acceleration.

Stronger heat requires explainable support across multiple independent evidence families.

A single price movement must not masquerade as broad attention acceleration.

### Principle 73 — 📦 Inventory Preserves Economic and Operational Identity

Inventory records represent economically and operationally comparable business positions. Spreadsheet row position is never identity. Product form, quantity meaning, operational treatment, and economic lineage must remain explainable.

### Principle 74 — 💰 Cost Follows Lineage

Inventory cost moves through splits, grading, and transformations rather than disappearing or duplicating. Missing cost remains unknown. Cost Layer history is authoritative over cosmetic averages.

### Principle 75 — 📍 Location, Custody, Exposure, and State Are Different Truths

MarketDEX must not collapse physical location, custody, platform exposure, or operational business state into one field or concept.

### Principle 76 — ⏱️ Capital Time Follows Capital Reality

Capital commitment time begins when business capital becomes committed and normally ends when attributable capital becomes reusable. Listing, grading, transformation, and other intermediate lifecycle events do not cosmetically reset the capital clock.

### Principle 77 — 🧬 Transformation Preserves Ancestry

When inventory is consumed, combined, separated, or materially changed into new operational identity, every materially contributing source must remain traceable through the authoritative Transformation Event to its results.

Lineage is permanent relationship history. MarketDEX must not reduce many-to-many ancestry to one cosmetic Parent ID field or permit circular lineage.

### Principle 78 — 🧮 Transformation Conserves Cost and Reconciles Quantity

Completed transformations must conserve attributable inventory cost.

Cost moves through Cost Layers and does not disappear or duplicate.

Quantity must reconcile using the applicable Product Form unit identity, but cross-form quantities must not be added together as false proof of conservation.

Remaining source quantity should derive from authoritative consumption evidence rather than become duplicate manual truth.

### Principle 79 — 📋 Known Contents and 🎲 Randomized Contents Are Different Truths

BREAK and OPEN are different transformation meanings.

BREAK reconciles known-content separation against applicable version-aware contents evidence.

OPEN reconciles randomized products against actual observed results rather than expected pull value.

Estimated bulk observations may remain visibly estimated until reconciled.

### Principle 80 — 📜 Completed Transformations Are Permanent History

A completed Transformation Event must not be silently rewritten or deleted to make later records appear correct.

Corrections, reversals, loss or damage adjustments, and exception outcomes must preserve linked corrective history.

Sold descendants and other completed downstream events remain historical facts.

### Principle 81 — 📦 Operational Grouping Must Not Invent Product Truth

Transformation Result Groups may simplify operational management only when grouped contents share intended operational treatment.

Detailed content evidence remains preserved.

Grouping must not create false product identity or hide materially different products merely to reduce workbook rows.

---

### Principle 82 — 👀 Reconciliation Observes Before It Corrects

A physical count is dated observation evidence and does not automatically replace authoritative inventory quantity.

Variance derives from Observed Quantity minus Recorded Quantity at Observation.

Unknown physical quantity remains unknown rather than becoming zero.

MarketDEX should investigate whether a Sale, Purchase, Transformation, Return, Transfer, or other authoritative business event explains a variance before using an Inventory Adjustment.

### Principle 83 — 🛠️ Inventory Adjustment Is a Last-Resort Authoritative Event

An Inventory Adjustment changes authoritative quantity only when verified physical reality differs and no more accurate business event should own the correction.

Adjustment Reason is required.

Direction and positive Adjustment Quantity determine signed movement.

A DECREASE consumes attributable Cost Layers. Version 0 uses FIFO by default for interchangeable quantity.

An INCREASE must use KNOWN COST, factually supported ZERO COST BASIS, or UNKNOWN COST. MarketDEX must not invent acquisition cost.

POSTED adjustments are permanent history and later reversal uses linked offsetting evidence.

### Principle 84 — 🔄 Recounts Preserve Observation History

Every recount creates a new Reconciliation Observation linked to the same Reconciliation Case.

Later observations may control resolution but do not rewrite earlier observations.

A verified MATCHED recount may resolve a case without an Adjustment Event.

A known missing business event should own the correction.

A verified unexplained physical difference may proceed to Inventory Adjustment.

Resolved cases may be reopened only with preserved prior resolution history.

### Principle 85 — 📊 Proven Business Logic Precedes Software Implementation

MarketDEX business logic should be understood and represented through the approved Spreadsheet Design responsibility before production software implementation begins.

The LibreOffice workbook remains the active proving ground and permanent business continuity system.

Future software should reduce friction around proven knowledge rather than invent business rules during coding.

If implementation exposes a contradiction in approved business logic, the contradiction returns to the appropriate planning responsibility before the locked logic changes.

### Principle 86 — 🎨 Prototype Visuals Explore Presentation, Not Business Truth

Prototype Design may explore how approved MarketDEX responsibilities, workflows, and information could appear in a future application.

A prototype does not become an authoritative business-rule source merely because a screen looks convincing.

Spreadsheet Design remains authoritative for workbook workflows and business logic.

Prototype Design may refine visual hierarchy, interaction concepts, navigation, and future application presentation without silently redefining approved business meaning.

### Principle 87 — 💻 Development Implements Proven Decisions

Development exists to implement approved MarketDEX knowledge.

Production implementation should originate from approved Spreadsheet Design or approved Prototype Design responsibilities.

Development must not redesign workflows, change business logic, or invent unrelated features while coding.

When implementation reveals a potentially better business rule or workflow, the idea returns to the appropriate planning responsibility for review before direction changes.

### Principle 88 — 🐞 Debugging Repairs Without Redesign

Debugging should identify root cause, apply the smallest safe fix, explain why the issue occurred, and verify the correction.

Bug repair must remain focused and isolated.

Debugging must not become a hidden architecture, roadmap, or feature-design process.

### Principle 89 — 📦 Releases Preserve Meaningful Evolution

Completed milestones, releases, packages, and important engineering decisions should be preserved as project history.

Release history documents what MarketDEX became and when.

Release tracking does not replace planning, implementation, or debugging responsibilities.
