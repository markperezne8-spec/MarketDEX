# Article I

## 🎯 Purpose

The MarketDEX Workbook is the primary active product and first working
edition of the MarketDEX business operating system.

It exists to organize business knowledge, support daily operations,
improve decision-making, preserve business continuity, and continuously
evolve through real-world experience.

The workbook is not temporary.

It must remain capable of helping operate the business independently
even if future software development pauses, changes direction, or fails.

Every worksheet should have a clear purpose.

Every worksheet should answer one or more meaningful business questions.

Every improvement should reduce unnecessary work while increasing
understanding.

# Article II

## 📦 Current Workbook Scope

The current active product categories are:

-   🃏 Pokémon TCG
-   🐉 Dragon Ball Card Game
-   🧸 Funko Pop

🎮 Gaming and additional collectible categories remain future
possibilities.

The workbook should use shared business structures whenever practical.

A separate worksheet should not be created for every product category

Category-specific fields, views, or worksheets should exist only when

Different collectibles.

One workbook.

## 🗄️ Historical Data Architecture

The workbook should be designed to preserve years of meaningful historical data.

Useful historical records should not be overwritten simply because a current value changes.

The workbook should distinguish between:

- 📍 Current State — what is true now.
- 📜 Event History — what happened and when.
- 📸 Snapshots — what important information looked like at a selected point in time.
- 🧠 Decision History — what was expected, assumed, or decided.
- 🌍 Outcomes — what actually happened.

**Why this matters:** years of preserved history allow MarketDEX to compare periods, identify repeating patterns, review decision quality, and develop greater understanding of market behavior.

Snapshots should be created only when historical comparison provides meaningful value.

# Article III

## 🧭 Workbook Architecture

The workbook should be organized around business responsibilities rather
than around technology.

The initial workbook modules are:

-   🏠 Mission Control
-   💰 Business
-   📦 Inventory
-   🃏 Collection
-   🧭 Market Intelligence
-   📊 Analytics
-   ⚙️ Administration

Each module may contain one or more worksheets.

A module is a business responsibility.

A worksheet is a focused business interface.

A worksheet should not become a dumping ground for unrelated
information.

The Business Questions document should guide worksheet creation.

The normal design flow is:

❓ Business Question

↓

🧩 Business Responsibility

↓

📊 Worksheet

↓

👀 Visual Understanding

↓

🎯 Decision or Action

# Article XII

## 📁 Workbook Design Rules

Every worksheet should:

-   🎯 Have one clear primary purpose.
-   ❓ Answer one or more meaningful business questions.
-   👀 Be easy to scan.
-   🧠 Reduce cognitive load.
-   🎯 Support a decision or action.
-   📁 Use authoritative information whenever practical.
-   🧩 Work with the rest of the workbook.
-   ✨ Prefer simplicity when two solutions provide similar value.
-   🌍 Improve when real-world experience proves the design weak.

Before adding a worksheet, ask:

> **What business question does this worksheet answer that the current
> workbook cannot answer clearly enough?**

If the answer is unclear, the worksheet should not be added yet.

Operational Status may include OPEN, WAITING FOR RETURN, UNDER REVIEW, RESOLVED, and CLOSED.

The event may create or link to financial adjustment, inventory adjustment, Capital State change, and final net outcome recalculation.

## 🏠 Mission Control Worksheet Contract — Attention and Capital Health

### 🎯 Primary Business Question

> **What deserves my attention today?**

Mission Control is primarily read-oriented.

It is not an Inventory editor, Purchase-entry sheet, Market-data entry sheet, Grading-entry sheet, Order-history warehouse, manually maintained KPI sheet, or chart museum.

### 🎯 Today's Top 3

Today's Top 3 consumes a hidden Attention Engine responsibility.

Candidate families may include Listing Leverage, Order Action, Business Exception, Capital Release, Market Opportunity, Market Risk, Seasonal Window, Release Readiness, Grading Action, Data Trust, and Inventory Reconciliation.

The ranking responsibility may consider Urgency, Capital Impact, Time Sensitivity, Actionability, Evidence Confidence, and Risk Exposure.

Mission Control displays rank, concise attention label, and **Why this is here** explanation.

Do not display a false-precision priority score.

Allowed lightweight interactions may include OPEN, SNOOZE, and DISMISS.

Attention Response History remains historical and hidden by default.

### 💰 Capital Health Zone

Top metric cards:

💵 Available Cash

🔄 Available for Redeployment

Trend visual:

📈 Reusable Capital Trend line chart.

Context indicator:

🚦 Capital Concentration Status using historical comparison when sufficient history exists.

Capital Health should not use one opaque Capital Health Score.

### 🧬 Capital Origin

Preserve capital origin so Owner Contribution and Borrowed Capital do not masquerade as business-cycle growth.

## 📦 Inventory Worksheet Planning — Checkpoint 015

**Status:** 🔒 DESIGN LOCKED THROUGH CAPITAL COMMITMENT CLOCK

### Inventory Identity

An Inventory Record represents one economically and operationally comparable business inventory position.

Multiple physical units may share one Inventory Record only when product identity, relevant product-form attributes, operational treatment, and economic lineage are sufficiently comparable.

Every Inventory Record uses a permanent Inventory Record ID. Worksheet row position is never identity.

Lots and collections preserve source lineage when split into child inventory positions.

### Product Form Taxonomy

Product Category, Product Form Group, and Product Form remain distinct responsibilities.

Product Form uses controlled vocabulary. Free-text Product Form entry is not authoritative.

Approved initial forms include RAW SINGLE, GRADED CARD, CARD LOT, BULK CARDS, ENERGY CARD, ETB, BOOSTER BOX, BOOSTER BUNDLE, BOOSTER PACK, COLLECTION BOX, TIN, BLISTER, BUILD & BATTLE, SEALED DECK / KIT, COLLECTION / PURCHASE LOT, ACCESSORY, PROMOTIONAL ITEM, and OTHER REVIEW.

Unknown or uncommon forms begin as OTHER REVIEW until real business use proves a meaningful operational distinction.

### Quantity + Unit Identity

Inventory Quantity counts the inventory unit defined by the applicable Product Form.

A managed Lot or acquired Collection may have Quantity 1 while preserving separate contained-item evidence.

BULK CARDS quantity represents individual card count and preserves VERIFIED or ESTIMATED count basis.

Unknown quantity remains unknown and must not silently become one or zero.

Current Quantity should derive from authoritative quantity-changing events where practical.

Quantity additions, sales, transformations, collection transfers, and reconciliation adjustments preserve history.

Inventory quantity must not silently become negative. Conflicting quantity evidence creates review.

### Cost Basis + Cost Layers

MarketDEX preserves acquisition cost through identifiable Cost Layers.

Landed Acquisition Cost includes purchase price and eligible attributable acquisition costs.

Shared acquisition costs use a preserved controlled allocation method. Purchase Value is the normal initial preset; Quantity, Weight, and reason-preserved Manual Allocation may be used where appropriate.

Cost follows lineage through lots, splits, grading, and transformations.

Cost moves rather than duplicates.

LibreOffice Version 0 uses FIFO Cost Layer consumption for partial quantity sales unless a later evidence-supported contract replaces it.

Unknown cost remains unknown and creates Cost Review Required.

Average Unit Cost may summarize a position but does not replace Cost Layer history.

### Physical Location + Custody

Physical Location, Custody, and Platform Exposure are separate business facts.

Physical storage uses controlled Storage Location identities.

Meaningful location and custody changes preserve history.

Returned inventory does not automatically re-enter sellable inventory before verification and condition review.

Conflicting authoritative location or custody evidence creates review.

### Inventory Business State

Business State identifies current operational availability.

Controlled initial states are RECEIVING, AVAILABLE, LISTED, RESERVED, GRADING, TRANSFORMATION, OUTBOUND, RETURN REVIEW, HOLD, and CLOSED.

SOLD is represented through authoritative sale and quantity-changing events rather than as a universal whole-record state.

LISTED should derive from active Platform Listing evidence where practical.

Divergent quantity treatment may require separate operational positions.

HOLD requires an identifiable reason.

Meaningful Business State changes preserve lifecycle history.

### Platform Exposure + Listing Relationship

Inventory Positions and Platform Listings remain separate but related records.

One Inventory Position may connect to zero, one, or many Platform Listings.

Cross-platform listings do not duplicate owned inventory quantity.

Owned Quantity, Reserved Quantity, and platform-specific Listed Quantity remain separate facts.

Aggregate listed exposure may exceed owned quantity when intentionally cross-listed.

Insufficient inventory for active listing exposure creates Listing Quantity Conflict review.

First Active Listing Date belongs to listing lifecycle evidence and must not silently reset because of cosmetic relisting, pausing, or refreshing.

### Inventory Age + Capital Commitment Clock

Inventory Age and Capital Commitment Time are separate measurements.

Capital Commitment Time begins when attributable business capital is authoritatively recognized as committed.

The capital clock normally continues through receiving, storage, listing, reservation, grading, transformation, outbound fulfillment, return review, and hold.

The capital clock normally ends when attributable capital becomes reusable again. Reliable Payout Received Date is the normal Capital Return Date for completed sales.

Sale Date does not automatically equal Capital Return Date.

Transformations and grading do not reset capital commitment history.

Partial quantity sales return only attributable capital supported by Cost Layer consumption and capital-return evidence.

Remaining inventory capital remains committed.

Returns and later adjustments preserve historical cycles and may create linked capital re-commitment evidence.

Missing commitment dates remain unknown and create Capital Age Review Required.

Estimated dates remain visibly identified as estimated.

## 🔗 Inventory Lineage + Transformation Contract — Design Locked

### 🔨 Transformation Boundary

A transformation exists only when source inventory is consumed, combined, separated, or materially changed and the resulting inventory requires new operational identity.

Location movement, Platform Exposure change, Inventory-to-Collection movement, and Sale are not transformations.

### 🧬 Lineage Relationship

Every resulting Inventory Position created through transformation must remain traceable to every authoritative source Inventory Position that materially contributed physical product, quantity, or cost.

The relationship pattern is:

`Source Inventory Record ID → Transformation Event ID → Result Inventory Record ID`

Lineage is relationship based and supports one-to-many, many-to-one, many-to-many, and one-to-one relationships.

Lineage is permanent history. Results must connect to their Transformation Event. Sources must be authoritative Inventory Records. Circular lineage is forbidden. Material contribution requires preserved lineage.

### 🔨 Transformation Event

A Transformation Event is the authoritative business event recording source inventory being intentionally consumed, separated, combined, or materially changed to create one or more resulting Inventory Positions with new operational identity.

Minimum responsibilities are Transformation Event ID, Transformation Type, Event Date, Event Status, Source Relationships, Result Relationships, Quantity Evidence, Cost Allocation Method, and Notes or Evidence.

Controlled statuses are PLANNED, IN PROGRESS, COMPLETED, CANCELLED, and REVIEW.

Controlled initial Transformation Types are BREAK, OPEN, SPLIT, COMBINE, GRADE, REPACKAGE, and OTHER REVIEW.

BREAK records known-content separation. OPEN records randomized-content opening controlled by actual observed results.

Only COMPLETED events change authoritative inventory quantity. CANCELLED events create no authoritative quantity or cost movement. Blocking quantity or cost conflicts require Transformation Review.

### 🔢 Source Consumption and Result Diversity

Every Transformation Source Relationship preserves Source Inventory Record ID and Quantity Consumed using the source Product Form's unit identity.

Remaining Source Quantity is derived and is not a second manually maintained truth.

One Transformation Event may create multiple Result Inventory Positions or controlled Result Groups with different Product Forms and Business States.

Cross-form quantities are never added together as proof of conservation.

SPLIT and COMBINE reconcile physical units where unit identity remains comparable.

### 💰 Transformation Cost Conservation

A completed transformation conserves attributable inventory cost.

Total attributable source cost consumed must equal total result cost allocated after controlled rounding, except separately recorded identifiable transformation costs.

Cost moves through Cost Layers and never duplicates.

Approved transformation allocation methods are PURCHASE VALUE, QUANTITY, WEIGHT, reason-preserved MANUAL ALLOCATION, and KNOWN CONTENT RELATIVE VALUE.

KNOWN CONTENT RELATIVE VALUE uses preserved relative-value evidence at the transformation date.

A result may receive zero allocated cost when controlled allocation evidence supports zero. MarketDEX must not invent nominal values merely to force a positive allocation.

Any controlled rounding residual is deterministically assigned to one identified result allocation and remains auditable.

Unknown cost remains unknown. Unexplained cost imbalance creates Cost Conservation Review Required.

### 📋 BREAK and 🎲 OPEN Reconciliation

Known-content BREAK results reconcile against the applicable version-aware Bill of Contents evidence.

Missing, unexpected, variant, or manufacturer-exception contents remain observable reconciliation evidence and may require review.

OPEN results reconcile against actual observed results rather than Expected Pull Value.

OPEN may create a controlled Bulk Result Group using ESTIMATED quantity evidence. Later physical reconciliation preserves correction history.

### 📦 Transformation Result Groups

Transformation results may use a controlled Result Group when materially similar contents are intentionally managed together as one operational Inventory Position.

Detailed contents remain preserved in Bill of Contents or applicable observation evidence.

A Result Group represents operational management rather than false product identity.

Separating grouped contents later creates a SPLIT transformation.

Allocated Cost Layers remain with the Result Group until attributable cost moves through a later event.

Materially different products must not be grouped merely to reduce rows.

Grouping is allowed only when contents share intended operational treatment.

### 🏆 Grading Lineage

GRADE preserves raw-card ancestry and normally consumes one attributable raw card to produce one attributable graded-card result or an explicit exception outcome.

Grading exception outcomes may include UNGRADED RETURN, ALTERED / AUTHENTICITY REVIEW, DAMAGE / LOSS, and OTHER REVIEW.

Detailed grading lifecycle responsibilities remain owned by Grading architecture. Transformation lineage does not create a competing grading system.

### 🛠️ Transformation Corrections, Reversals, and Exceptions

Completed Transformation Events are permanent historical evidence and are not silently rewritten or deleted.

PLANNED and IN PROGRESS events may be edited before authoritative inventory movement finalizes.

Corrective event families are CORRECTION, REVERSAL, LOSS / DAMAGE ADJUSTMENT, and EXCEPTION OUTCOME.

Every corrective event references the affected Transformation Event ID. Correction Reason is required. Correction Event Date remains separate from the original Event Date.

Corrective quantity and Cost Layer movement remains traceable. REVERSAL does not erase original history and MarketDEX must not assume every transformation is physically reversible.

Loss and damage remain explicit outcomes rather than hidden shrinkage.

Sold descendants remain permanent historical facts. A later correction creates linked offsetting quantity or Cost Layer adjustment evidence where required and does not rewrite the completed sale.

Repeated transformation corrections become Data Quality evidence.

### ⚠️ Blocking Reviews

Unexplained quantity conflict creates Transformation Quantity Review Required.

Unexplained cost imbalance creates Cost Conservation Review Required.

Incomplete, conflicting, circular, or broken lineage creates Lineage Review Required.

A Transformation Event cannot become COMPLETED while a blocking conservation conflict remains unresolved.

### ⚙️ LibreOffice Version 0 Protection

Inventory remains a simple operational summary.

Transformation Events, Transformation Source Relationships, Transformation Result Relationships, lineage evidence, and Cost Layers belong to dedicated warehouse or calculation responsibilities rather than being flattened into the visible Inventory worksheet.

Mark records the real business event once. Remaining quantities, lineage summaries, cost checks, and blocking reviews should derive automatically wherever LibreOffice Calc safely permits.

Version 0 should be stress-tested with realistic transformation volume before workbook implementation is considered mature.

## 🔍 Inventory Reconciliation + Adjustment Contract — Design Locked

### ⚖️ Boundary

Reconciliation compares recorded inventory truth with observed physical reality.

An Adjustment is the authoritative quantity-changing event used to correct a verified physical difference only when no more accurate Sale, Purchase, Transformation, Return, Transfer, or other business event should own the change.

Physical Count is observation, not automatic inventory truth.

Variance is derived as `Observed Quantity − Recorded Quantity at Observation`.

Only an authoritative quantity-changing event changes inventory quantity.

### 📋 Reconciliation Observation

A Reconciliation Observation is dated evidence of what Mark physically observed when checking one authoritative Inventory Record.

Minimum responsibilities are Reconciliation Observation ID, Reconciliation Case ID, Observation Date, Inventory Record ID, Storage Location checked, Recorded Quantity at Observation, Observed Quantity, Count Basis, derived Variance, Observation Status, and Notes or Evidence.

Controlled Count Basis values are VERIFIED and ESTIMATED.

Controlled Observation Statuses are MATCHED, VARIANCE REVIEW, CAUSE IDENTIFIED, ADJUSTMENT REQUIRED, and RESOLVED.

Unknown Observed Quantity remains unknown. ESTIMATED remains visibly estimated. Observed Quantity uses the Inventory Record's locked unit identity.

Recorded Quantity at Observation is preserved as historical comparison evidence.

### 🛠️ Inventory Adjustment Event

An Inventory Adjustment Event is the authoritative quantity-changing event used to correct a verified physical inventory difference when no more accurate business event should own the change.

Minimum responsibilities are Adjustment Event ID, Adjustment Date, Inventory Record ID, linked Reconciliation Observation ID when applicable, Adjustment Direction, Adjustment Quantity, Adjustment Reason, Cost Treatment, Evidence or Notes, and Adjustment Status.

Controlled Adjustment Directions are INCREASE and DECREASE. Adjustment Quantity is entered as a positive magnitude.

Controlled Adjustment Reasons are COUNT ERROR, UNRECORDED LOSS, UNRECORDED DAMAGE, FOUND INVENTORY, LEGACY DATA CORRECTION, and OTHER REVIEW.

Controlled Adjustment Statuses are DRAFT, REVIEW, POSTED, and REVERSED.

Only POSTED adjustments change authoritative quantity.

DECREASE consumes attributable Cost Layers. FIFO remains the Version 0 default for interchangeable quantity.

INCREASE uses KNOWN COST, ZERO COST BASIS when factually supported, or UNKNOWN COST. UNKNOWN COST creates Cost Review Required. MarketDEX must not invent acquisition cost.

Quantity or cost conflicts block POSTED status.

### 🔄 Reconciliation Case + Recount Resolution

Reconciliation Case ID is the lightweight grouping identity connecting observations, recounts, investigation, and resolution for the same inventory discrepancy.

Every recount creates a new Reconciliation Observation linked to the same case.

Earlier observations remain permanent.

A later observation may supersede an earlier observation for resolution purposes but never rewrites the earlier count.

The preferred resolution flow is:

`OBSERVE → INVESTIGATE → RECOUNT → IDENTIFY CAUSE or ADJUST → RESOLVE`

MATCHED may resolve a case without an Adjustment Event.

CAUSE IDENTIFIED resolves through the correct authoritative business event.

ADJUSTMENT REQUIRED resolves only after the applicable Adjustment Event reaches POSTED.

Unknown or estimated observations cannot silently prove a verified exact match.

Temporary custody or location differences should be investigated before quantity adjustment.

Resolution Method and Resolution Date are preserved.

Reopening a resolved case preserves prior resolution history.

Repeated variances, recounts, reopened cases, and Adjustment Reasons become Data Quality evidence.

### ⚙️ Version 0 Protection

Inventory remains an operational summary.

Reconciliation Cases, Reconciliation Observations, and Inventory Adjustment Events belong to dedicated warehouse or calculation responsibilities.

Mark records the physical observation once. Variance, review conditions, and applicable summaries should derive automatically wherever LibreOffice Calc safely permits.

Mission Control may surface unresolved reconciliation and adjustment review conditions but does not become the adjustment editor.
